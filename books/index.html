Books

Here’s a list of some of the books I’ve read over the past few years. For my favorites, I’ve also added a brief comment. If you love reading and would like to discuss what you’ve read, feel free to reach out to me. I always enjoy sharing thoughts and exchanging opinions about literature.

Pride and Prejudice - Jane Austen
Emma - Jane Austen
I see a part of Emma reflected in me—her vanity and her flaws, yet also the warmth of her heart. She remains one of my favourite characters of all time.
Jane Eyre - Charlotte Brontë
Northanger Abbey - Jane Austen
Mansfield Park - Jane Austen
Sense and Sensibility - Jane Austen
Persuasion - Jane Austen
Lady Susan, The Watsons, Sanditon - Jane Austen
A room of one's own - Virginia Woolf
This book opened my mind like no other, and I always recommend it to anyone who enjoys a good essay. It even inspired the title of this website.
Mrs Dalloway - Virginia Woolf
The Bell Jar - Sylvia Plath
The Catcher in the Rye (Il giovane Holden) - J.D. Salinger
Sometimes I think I could write something like this. You can easily share the protagonist's point of view.
Lord of the Flies - William Golding
A very disturbing book.
Madame Bovary - Gustave Flaubert
It gave me the impression that the writer wasn’t very familiar with women.
Little Woman, Good Wives - Louisa May Alcott
Jo March is my everyday inspiration.
The Woodlanders - Thomas Hardy
Summer - Edith Wharton
Very interesting for anyone who wants to understand more about the condition of women during that time.
Wuthering Heights - Emily Brontë
How is it possible to both love and hate a character?
Frankenstein - Mary Shelley
Saddest book of all time.
The Picture of Dorian Gray - Oscar Wilde
1984 - George Orwell
A Farewell to Arms - Ernest Hemingway
This book made me cry a river of tears.
Fiesta: The Sun Also Rises - Ernest Hemingway
Sometimes I wish I could live like Hemingway.
The Old Man and the Sea - Ernest Hemingway
Carmilla - J. Sheridan Le Fanu
